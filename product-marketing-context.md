## Who We Serve

### Ideal Customer Profile

**Primary:** Homeowners aged 35–65 in suburban Pittsburgh communities who:
- Take pride in their property but don't have time to maintain it themselves
- Have invested in their home and want it to reflect that investment
- Value reliability and consistent quality over the cheapest option
- Prefer working with a local, owner-operated business they can trust

**Secondary:** Small commercial properties, HOAs, and property managers who
need dependable, professional service on a recurring schedule.

### Customer Pain Points
- Can't trust contractors to show up consistently
- Tired of flaky, unprofessional services
- Frustrated by unclear pricing and surprise charges
- Want someone who knows their property and remembers their preferences
- Concerned about damage to expensive plants or hardscape during service

### What Customers Are Really Buying
Not lawn care — **peace of mind**. The confidence that their property looks
great without having to think about it.

## Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice
**Confident, local, straightforward.**

We know what we're doing. We've been doing it here for 15 years. We say
what we mean, show up when we say we will, and do the work right the first
time.

### Tone Guidelines
- **Do:** Speak directly. Use "we" and "you." Be specific.
- **Do:** Reference local context naturally — neighborhood names, seasonal
  conditions, common property types.
- **Do:** Lead with outcomes ("your lawn will look its best") not features
  ("we use commercial-grade equipment").
- **Don't:** Use filler words like "world-class," "second to none,"
  "passionate about," or "cutting-edge."
- **Don't:** Sound like a corporate press release. We're a local business.
- **Don't:** Oversell. Let the work speak.

### Sample Voice
✓ "We've been maintaining properties in Wexford for over a decade. We know
   the soil, we know the seasons, and we know what actually works here."

✗ "Our passionate team of world-class professionals is dedicated to
   delivering industry-leading solutions for all your landscaping needs."

## Content Themes

These are the topics we return to regularly in blog posts, social content,
and page copy:

1. **Local know-how** — What works in Pittsburgh that doesn't work elsewhere,
   and vice versa
2. **Before/after transformations** — Project showcases that make the value
   tangible
3. **Homeowner education** — Tips that help customers make better decisions
   and position us as the trusted expert
4. **Behind the scenes** — The crew, the process, the standards we hold
   ourselves to
5. **Seasonal prep** — Timely, useful content tied to what homeowners are
   thinking about right now

## What to Avoid in All Copy

- Vague superlatives with no support ("best," "top-rated," "premier")
- Generic stock-photo language ("transform your outdoor living space")
- Anything that sounds like it was written by AI for no specific audience
- Jargon that homeowners don't use
- Promising outcomes we can't guarantee
